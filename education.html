    Why I Chose Information Technology

    I chose to study Information Technology because since high school, I've developed a love of computers and everything about them, the hardware and software, network communications, programming and a whole lot more that was introduced to me in school. I knew that if studied IT, it would give me a taste of every part of the IT industry, not just a single aspect, and I knew it would help me understand what part of the industry I would enjoy the most and work towards for a career.

    I've decided to pursue a Major in Networks and Security, as I've always been fascinated about how computers are able to communicate data from one another, and at such long distances in a split second. Getting the opportunity to learn all about networking, and how security plays a part in keeping data safe, is a great opportunity for me, and I hope to use it as a pathway for a career in networking and security. Unfortunately, Griffith does not seem to supply any minors for IT, so I am unfortunately unable to complete one.

    Certificate III in Education Support (TEAMS)

    Experience and Outcome

    After Graduating from High school, I was given the opportunity to be employed by the same school as a IT trainee for one year, and attached to that traineeship was this Certificate III of Education Support.

    To complete this certificate, it required me to enter active classrooms and monitor, assist and gain a deeper understanding of both students and teachers in the classroom. Furthermore, I was required to complete a range of questions relating to the subject of supporting the classroom in assistance of the teacher, with safety questions, conduct questions, and plenty more.

    I completed these questions to a good result within the year that I was instructed to do so. Also, inbetween completing my duties of an IT trainee, I would be sent into certain classrooms to complete the above requirements, and in turn, I would be lead by the teacher for how I should interact with the students, and what was required of me. The result of the Certificate was a success in allowing me to develop the skills required within the classroom, and the knowledge to keep the classroom a safe and secure place. Furthermore, I've developed personal attributes of being assertive, and confident in my decision, especially in the classroom, and develop the skill to understand how others are feeling and thinking within a learning environment.

    Expected outcomes from receiving Certificate

    This qualification reflects the role of workers who assist teachers and support student learning in a range of classroom settings. They complete general administrative as well as operational tasks to support students with learning under the guidance of a teacher or other educational professional. Work requires use of discretion and judgement within the boundaries of established policies and procedures.

    To achieve this qualification the candidate must complete a total of at least 100 hours of work in a classroom environment catering to primary or secondary school students, within at least one school in Australia as detailed in the Assessment Requirements of units of competency.
