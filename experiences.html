    Industry Knowlege and Engagement

    IT Traineeship

    In 2019, my high school opened up an opportunity that I could not refuse. During my year 12 school year, multiple different one year traineeships were offered for our year, with one of them being an IT traineeship. Being the only one to apply for it, I was automatically awarded the position, as well as a certificate to go with it. This was extremely lucky for me, because I had screwed up my course selection, and I was short a subject, and resulted in me being disqualified from receiving an ATAR in NSW, and in turn, I could not go to university after school. Fortunately, this traineeship was able to give me the pathway I needed to start my study in IT.

    During this traineeship, my main job was to provide technical support for teachers and students, allowing me to get real world experience for the IT industry, and experience all kind of requirements that I needed for the industry. These included skills, like communication, problem solving, a good work ethic, and also included required extensive knowledge on both computer hardware and software, and some knowledge on networking

    In the end, I came out of the traineeship with a developed understanding of the field of technical support and an improved set of skills that are required for the IT industry, which has helped me significantly in my studies at university.

    Developing a Game - Creative Coding Assignment

    During the course "Creating Coding" at Griffith University, I was given a introduction to JavaScript through using the functions library "P5.js". From the course, we learned how to use JavaScript’s in-built functions library, as well as the P5.js's library to create interactive programs for the user. The final assignment required the class to recreate "Missile Defence", using only JavaScript and P5.js.

    This assignment was extremely fun, and the way that I wanted to recreate the game, was by figuring out as many functions as I could from watching gameplay, then trying to recreate the functions as accurately as I could. Although I did not have the proper expertise, or time to implement all the functions, I did what I could, and created a game that is reminiscent of Missile Defence. With not being as advanced, but feels and plays like the original game. My intentions were to create a more functional and interesting game, rather than a visually good looking one.

    This experience was extremely important for my developing programming skill, as I was able see my flaws in my code, and what I could have done better. But also it helped grow other skills like problem solving, which was required quite often in the making of the game.
